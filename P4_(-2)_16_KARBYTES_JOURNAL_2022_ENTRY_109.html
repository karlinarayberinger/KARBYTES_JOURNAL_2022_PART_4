I intend for this journal entry to be the last one in this chapter. I will try to make it succinct.

To make a long story short, I have been high on THC most days throughout the past three years (if not longer). What being high on marijuana has done for me is attain interesting “angles” on reality (especially the experience that my reality is fundamentally holographic; hollow and luminous). What being high on marijuana has also done for me is shorten my attention span (which caused me to feel mentally claustrophobic because my short term memory wanted to encompass more content than it had resources for). In short, I blame myself 100% for my slowness to get a job and to become financially independent. At the same time, I cannot help but be grateful for the fact that being perpetually high gave me a convenient and effective way to isolate myself from other people and to buy myself more time to inhabit my own solipsistic little world.

There are other drugs I would like to get my hands on besides marijuana for the purpose of expanding my consciousness. I have already tried magic mushrooms three times (and I purchased them legally at a shroomery in Oakland). I intend to attempt to buy LSD and DMT off the dark net using a virtual private network and the onion router to “hide” my activity (and I do not mind gaining notoriety for going public about such antics). Rather than risk wasting my money and time going for that black market stuff this year, I plan to just stick to weed and shrooms since those are the “tried and true” safe substances to acquire and use. I want to be more careful with how I use shrooms so that I do not waste the trip on some stupid psychological matter such as not having finished my homework assignments on time. I think it is wise to do shrooms no more than once per week at most (and averaging about twice per month) because I need at least a few days to process the experience. What I like about shrooms is that they force me to confront what is really bothering me and what I am fooling myself into postponing having to deal with. I have already confronted some of my most dreaded thoughts while on shrooms, but I know there is always more to delve into.

In my previous journal entry, I went a bit overboard complaining about other people being stupider, meaner, and more complacent than I am. Even the police who monitor my activities and bark at me “telepathically” are not as bad as I described. I can tell that their intentions are mostly benevolent. I am grateful for their intervention. They helped me not be so complacent, weak, lazy, close minded, and dumb. They literally force fed me news because I was almost certainly not going to get around to acquiring such facts on my own. I do have a natural tendency to avoid going out of my comfort zone. That’s not to say I do not sometimes pine for those voices to stop so badly I could scream and break a window. Thankfully, I have not dealt serious damage as a result of feeling mentally suffocated by the voices of others incessantly talking inside of my head.

Well, I am back at that cozy little nook at that church. The sky is overcast. After the sun set this evening and the sky was not yet overcast, I saw the clouds moving very quickly from west to east. The wind speeds must have been very high. It might rain tonight. That’s good. I am prepared. A little rain would be refreshing. I am glad the temperature is lower now. It’s actually a bit chilly. I would rather the temperature be very cold than very warm because I can always put more clothes on. In hot weather, there is little I can do to escape the oppressive heat if I am trapped outside where shade is scant. Well, I got that mostly figured out. I know where the good shade spots are which are within walking distance of my house.

Speaking of my house, I decided to skip going there to take a shower. I’m not that dirty. It has not been that long since I showered and did laundry. It is nice spending lots of time outside without going home because it gives me lots of uninterrupted time to think. My mom called me on the phone shortly before I got on the train to go back to Castro Valley. It was nice to have my mom check on me and say nice things to me. I was feeling a bit down at the time.

Well, I do not feel so down now. In fact, I feel delighted. I am giddy with hope and joy and appreciation for what my life is.

If I end up working at a movie theater concession stand looking like a teenager, that is fine. I no longer wish to take life so damned seriously. Earlier today I was bitching and moaning about how the 510 area is poor and “ghetto” compared to the 415 and 925 areas (but all of those areas have their cede elements). Poverty is inescapable everywhere I go. It is something that cannot be ignored. I do not want to ignore it anyhow. I feel motivated on a deep level to help everyone live as well as possible. Sadly, as soon as I advertise that I want to make healthy living, ongoing education, recreation, and decent housing accessible to all people I get sniped at by the voices and reminded that I am promoting myself as a humanitarian rather than seriously investing myself in helping other people. Being unemployed is not that much of a hindrance to me being an informed and contributing citizen. If I wanted to, I could be a lot more well read on social issues and a lot more involved in volunteering for humanitarian causes. I just have not wanted to because, in the past when I did, I got sniffed out as some chauvinist pretending to be angelic. Apparently, I was (and still am) too much of a self-centered, self-indulgent, elitist, small-minded, anti-social pig to be taken seriously as a humanitarian. So be it! Perhaps it is best for me to just focus on the stuff I am interested in rather than try to fit in where I do not belong.

Honestly, I do have contempt for those “social justice warrior” types who do little more than complain, cry, and protest about social justice issues while demonizing so-called elitists and left-brain dominant types who would rather avoid getting riled up over social justice issues in favor of staying focused on solving problems as engineers and scientists. I would much rather identify as and hang out with fellow science majors than with humanities majors. There! Time to go disappear into a wormhole. Good night!
